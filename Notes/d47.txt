OBJECTIVE
---------
D42 failed at the data collection stage: the expert driver averaged only 17 steps
per episode (884 total samples) because `target_speed = max_speed - corner_factor × |err|`
went negative at Monaco's large initial heading errors → stall → crash.

D47 fixes the expert driver params so BC data collection succeeds, then re-runs
the full Monaco pipeline (collect → BC → curriculum PPO).

WHAT CHANGED VS D42
-------------------
expert/collect_data.py → generate_monaco_dataset():

    OLD (D42): max_speed=17.0, corner_factor=12.0, lookahead=8
    NEW (D47): max_speed=6.0,  corner_factor=3.0,  lookahead=1

Root cause diagnosis:
  - D42's lookahead=8 waypoints on Monaco = 8 × 12.5m/pt = 100m ahead.
    The steering correction for a 1m lateral offset at 100m lookahead is
    atan(1/100) ≈ 0.010 rad — far too small to correct drift on a straight.
    The car drifted ~0.05m/step until it exceeded the 3m termination boundary.
  - The key fix is lookahead=1 (12.5m ahead) — correction = atan(1/12.5) = 0.080 rad,
    5× larger, sufficient for Monaco's tight track profile.
  - max_speed and corner_factor reductions provide extra safety margin.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
-------------------
The STAGES curriculum was designed for the oval track. Stage 0 requires
grad_lap_rate=0.5 (50% of episodes must complete ≥1 lap) before advancing.

Monaco geometry makes this impossible:
  - Monaco circumference: ~3750m (300 wpts × 12.5m/pt)
  - At Stage 0 max_accel=6 m/s² with BC init at ~6 m/s, the agent drives
    at moderate speed but still crashes at tight hairpins
  - ep_len_mean = 514 steps → distance covered ≈ 514 × 0.1s × ~6 m/s ≈ 308m
  - 308m / 3750m = 8.2% of one lap — far from the 50% lap completion needed
  - After 3M steps the agent converged to a fixed crashing pattern at ~500 steps

Why the BC init doesn't help enough:
  - BC was trained on max_speed=6 m/s data (lookahead=1, reactive steering)
  - Stage 0 curriculum allows max_accel=6 m/s² → car can go faster than 6 m/s
  - At higher speeds, lookahead=1 steering behavior (trained at 6 m/s) overshoots
    corners → instability → crash

WHAT'S NEEDED FOR D49 (MONACO V3)
------------------------------------
The fundamental issue is that STAGES curriculum is too aggressive for Monaco.
Options:

Option A — Monaco-specific curriculum with lower speed limits:
  Stage 0: max_accel=2, max_speed_soft=8, grad_lap_rate=0.2 (20% completion OK)
  Stage 1: max_accel=4, grad_lap_rate=0.1
  Stage 2: max_accel=8, grad_lap_rate=0 (never graduates, train to budget)

Option B — Skip curriculum entirely, PPO fine-tune from BC at fixed max_accel:
  - No CurriculumCallback
  - Set car.max_accel=8 (moderate, not too fast for Monaco)
  - Run 5M steps directly
  - The BC policy already drives at ~6 m/s — PPO just needs to optimize

Option C — Reduce max_steps from 6000 to 3000 so the graduation criterion is
  for partial laps (easier to satisfy), then increase later.

Recommended: Option B (simplest, avoids curriculum hyperparameter tuning).
Expected outcome: policy learns to navigate Monaco hairpins at ~8 m/s,
completing full laps. Curvature weights should remain non-zero or increase.
