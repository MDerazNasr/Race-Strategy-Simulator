ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ROOT CAUSE: Loading d21 into the pit_timing_reward env triggers a value
recalibration crisis that destroys the driving policy.

HOW IT UNFOLDED:
  1. D21 was trained on the standard pit env (pit_timing_reward=False).
     Its value function was calibrated for that reward structure.

  2. Loading d21 into pit_timing_reward=True changes the reward landscape:
     - Voluntary pit at tyre_life < 0.30: net cost -100 (instead of -200)
     - Early pit at tyre_life > 0.50: net cost -300 (instead of -200)
     - These changes mean the value function is WRONG at the start.

  3. PPO detects the large value error → large gradient updates → crisis.
     ep_rew_mean = -1470, ep_len collapses.

  4. During the crisis, the gradient signal is dominated by crashes.
     "Avoid the crash at step 23" becomes the strongest signal.
     The steer output converges toward the mean action that statistically
     avoids crashes across the distribution of stochastic episodes.

  5. But the MEAN action (deterministic evaluation) is wrong: hard left
     from step 1, which crashes every time. Stochastic training works because
     high throttle variance explores actions that avoid this crash — but
     the mean is degenerate.

  6. Once steer std collapses to 0.144, the deterministic policy is locked
     into hard-left. The stochastic policy occasionally avoids this by
     chance, explaining why ep_rew_mean=842 in training.

WHY D24 FAILED WORSE THAN D23:
  D23 also loaded d21 into the pit_timing_reward env and had the same crisis
  (ep_rew_mean = -1760 at 1.1M). But D23 recovered to driving well (2174 reward,
  8 laps). Why?

  D24 had TWO additional gradient signals that D23 did not have:
    1. Safety net forced pits (threshold=0.25 in Stage 0): fires when tyre_life
       < 0.25, adding a forced-pit reward signal on top of the timing bonus.
    2. STAGES_PIT_V5 curriculum transitions (Stage 0→1→2): env threshold changes
       mid-training, causing the value function to re-recalibrate THREE TIMES.

  The additional signals from the safety net + curriculum transitions may have
  deepened or prolonged the crisis, preventing driving recovery that d23 achieved.

  D23 had a simpler optimization landscape (no safety net, no curriculum stages):
  just "drive, and if you pit voluntarily, get timing bonus/penalty." The cleaner
  signal let driving recover. D24's complex multi-signal environment made recovery
  harder.

THE FUNDAMENTAL BARRIER:
  Any fine-tuning from d21 into the pit_timing_reward env causes value
  recalibration. During recalibration, the gradient updates are large and
  can destroy driving in ways that stochastic training masks but deterministic
  evaluation reveals.

  The two attempts to solve this:
    D23: Freeze pit output row → mlp_extractor still changes → driving recovers
         but pitting forgotten (mlp_extractor drifted away from pit signal)
    D24: Safety net curriculum → value crisis destroys steering confidence →
         deterministic policy collapses to hard-left turn from step 1

WHAT D25 MUST DO DIFFERENTLY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two root causes to fix simultaneously:
  1. Value recalibration crisis: loading d21 into pit_timing_reward env
  2. Driving regression: large gradient updates during crisis destroy steering

PROPOSED D25 APPROACH:
  Fix 1 — Use the SAME env as d21 (no pit_timing_reward):
    D21 was trained on standard pit env (pit_timing_reward=False).
    Loading it into the SAME env means NO value recalibration crisis.
    The reward structure is unchanged → no large initial gradient → no crisis.
    Evidence: d22 (standard env, 2M steps) had NO crisis → recovered to 2283 reward.

  Fix 2 — Freeze mlp_extractor.policy_net entirely:
    The driving behavior lives in mlp_extractor.policy_net (hidden features).
    Freezing ALL of these parameters prevents any driving regression.
    Only action_net (the 3D output head) can change:
      action_net.weight[0,:], action_net.bias[0] → throttle (can refine or freeze)
      action_net.weight[1,:], action_net.bias[1] → steer (can refine or freeze)
      action_net.weight[2,:], action_net.bias[2] → pit (CAN improve pit timing)
    With frozen driving features, the deterministic policy is GUARANTEED to
    preserve d21's driving behavior (features → actions is a fixed linear mapping
    over the frozen features).

  Combine with STAGES_PIT_V5:
    Safety net at 0.25/0.15/0.08 keeps pit experiences in the distribution.
    Even if the agent's voluntary pitting drifts, the safety net catches it.

  Expected outcome:
    - Driving: PRESERVED (mlp_extractor frozen → same features as d21)
    - Pitting: MAINTAINED (safety net keeps pit experiences in distribution)
    - Timing: IMPROVED? (action_net free to shift pit_signal threshold)
    - Upper bound: at best, d21's driving quality (1877 reward, 7 laps) with
      improved pit timing. Cannot exceed d21 on driving since features are frozen.

  The cost: cannot improve driving speed/accuracy. This is acceptable since
  our goal is to FIX pit timing, not to improve driving further.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LESSONS LEARNED
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Never load d21 into pit_timing_reward env without freezing the driving features.
   The reward change forces value recalibration, which destroys the policy.

2. "Training ep_rew_mean looks good" doesn't mean "deterministic evaluation is good."
   Stochastic policies can mask degenerate deterministic means.
   ALWAYS run deterministic evaluation during training (check every N steps).

3. The safety net idea is SOUND but was combined with the wrong env change.
   Safety net + standard env (no pit_timing_reward) + frozen driving features
   should work without triggering the crisis.

4. Steer confidence collapse (std → 0.14) can happen before total entropy
   collapse. Check individual dimension stds, not just mean std.
