WHAT WAS BUILT
==============================================================================

New standalone environment: F1MultiAgentEnv (env/f1_multi_env.py)
  - Ego car (PPO-controlled, DynamicCar) vs opponent (ExpertDriver, max_speed=22 m/s)
  - 13D observation: [11D standard ego obs] + [track_gap] + [opp_speed_norm]
  - 2D action: [throttle, steer] — no pit signal
  - New reward terms:
      position_bonus   = +0.5/step when track_gap < 0 (ego ahead)
      overtake_bonus   = +200 one-time when ego transitions from behind to ahead
      overtake_cooldown = 200 steps (prevents lapping from farming the bonus)
      collision_penalty= -0.5/step within 3.0m of opponent

Track gap definition:
  track_gap = (opp_idx - ego_idx) / len(track), wrap-corrected to [-0.5, 0.5]
  Positive = opponent ahead. Negative = ego ahead.

Reset:
  Fixed start: ego at waypoint 0 (v=5 m/s, aligned), opponent at waypoint 20 (slightly ahead)
  Random start: ego at random waypoint, opponent at (ego_idx + 20) % N

Training:
  - Start: ppo_curriculum_v2.zip (11D) extended to 13D via extend_obs_dim + buffer recreation
  - LR: cosine 1e-4 → 1e-6
  - ent_coef = 0.01 FROM THE START (lesson from d38: prevent log_std collapse)
  - Full unfreeze: 36,997 trainable params
  - 3M steps, DummyVecEnv([make_env_multi_agent])

Key implementation fixes required:
  1. extend_obs_dim sets new dim bounds as low=0.0 → manually fix dim 11 (track_gap) to low=-1.0
  2. PPO.load without env pre-creates rollout buffer at 11D → must recreate RolloutBuffer(13D) before set_env
  3. model.n_envs stays at 1 after load — DummyVecEnv([factory] * 1) avoids n_envs mismatch

==============================================================================
KEY FINDING: SPEED ADVANTAGE, NOT STRATEGIC AWARENESS
==============================================================================

The zero weights on track_gap (col[11]) and opp_speed_norm (col[12]) reveal that
D39 never developed explicit opponent awareness. Instead, it relies entirely on
raw speed advantage inherited from cv2 (26.91 m/s ego vs 22 m/s opponent).

Why this happened:
  1. cv2's driving (26.9 m/s) is already ~22% faster than the opponent (22 m/s).
  2. Being faster is sufficient to overtake without needing to "see" the opponent.
  3. The position_bonus (+0.5/step when ahead) is dominated by the base driving reward.
  4. PPO never needed to update col[11]/col[12] weights — the gradient through those
     columns was zero since track_gap didn't change the policy's actions.

This is correct behavior for a faster car against a slower opponent. The 5 overtakes
per episode are the ego lapping around the 22 m/s ExpertDriver ~5 times in 17 laps.

For genuine strategic racing (blocking, position defense, drafting), the opponent
would need to be FASTER than the ego (max_speed ≥ 28 m/s) so the speed advantage
alone is insufficient to win.

  - 26.91 m/s speed (vs cv2's 26.92 m/s — essentially identical)

GAINED vs single-agent:
  - Competitive framing: reward now reflects race position, not just solo time
  - 5 overtakes per episode (demonstrates gap closures)
  - ~33% reward increase (6025 vs 4531) from position/overtake bonuses

LIMITATION:
  - New dimensions (track_gap, opp_speed_norm) carry zero learned weight
  - No true position defense or strategic blocking behavior
  - Must set opponent max_speed > ego speed (≥28 m/s) for strategic behavior
