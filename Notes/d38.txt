WHAT WAS ATTEMPTED
==============================================================================

D37 converged to a local optimum with collapsed log_std:
  steer std: 0.57 (d36) → 0.05 (d37)  ← nearly deterministic
  throttle std: 0.77 (d36) → 0.18 (d37)

With std≈0.05 for steer, the policy drives a fixed line and cannot explore
faster trajectories. To unlock lap 16 (requiring ~26.9 m/s vs current 23.67 m/s),
exploration is needed.

D38 approach: load d37, set ent_coef=0.01 (SB3 default is 0.0).
The entropy coefficient adds a bonus to the PPO objective:
  Total loss = PPO_clip_loss + value_loss − ent_coef × H(π)
Since SB3 minimizes the total loss, maximizing H(π) (entropy) is a bonus.
For a Gaussian policy, ∂H/∂log_std = 1 for each dim → pushes log_std UP.
Expected effect: steer std rises from 0.05 back toward 0.3-0.5, restoring
exploration and potentially finding faster driving lines.

==============================================================================
ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
==============================================================================

The entropy bonus destabilized the driving policy before faster lines were found.

Timeline of failure:
  1. D37's steer std = 0.05: the policy drives a precise, narrow line.
     Any deviation from this line (added noise from entropy boost) risks going off-track.
  2. Entropy bonus adds noise to ALL action dimensions — throttle, steer, AND pit.
     The steer noise (even small: 0.05→0.079) perturbs the carefully tuned trajectory.
  3. The policy tries to adapt to the new, noisier action distribution.
     It finds a DIFFERENT local optimum with lower performance.
  4. The driving trajectory now crashes at lap 7 — the same point where d21-d31 crashed
     before the full-unfreeze improvement. The entropy boost effectively un-did d36's
     driving improvement.
  5. Pit std also increased (0.71→1.047), making pit decisions more erratic:
     only 1 pit in fixed-start vs 3 pits for d37. The pit strategy degraded too.

Why ent_coef=0.01 from d37 specifically failed (vs from d36):
  D37's log_std was already collapsed. The policy lived in a very narrow basin.
  Adding entropy to a collapsed policy is like adding noise to a precisely tuned
  system — it breaks the precision before the policy can adapt to find something better.
  If ent_coef=0.01 had been applied from d36 (where std was still 0.57 for steer),
  the baseline exploration was higher and the policy might have been more robust
  to the entropy-induced perturbations.

==============================================================================
LESSON LEARNED
==============================================================================

"Restoring entropy after collapse is harder than preventing collapse in the first place."

D38 tried to fix the log_std collapse AFTER it happened. This didn't work because:
  — The policy was in a narrow local optimum with near-deterministic steering
  — Adding exploration noise perturbed it out of that optimum to a worse place
  — The better approach: PREVENT the collapse (set ent_coef=0.01 from d36, or earlier)

For future work:
  — If starting a new training run from d36: use ent_coef=0.01 from the beginning
  — Monitor log_std during training; if steer std drops below 0.2, add entropy
  — Alternative: use a minimum std floor (clip log_std to >= -1.6 → std >= 0.2)
