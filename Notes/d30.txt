==============================================================================
ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
==============================================================================

THE EARLY OVER-EXPLORATION PROBLEM:
  D30 started from d21 (pit_bias=+0.006, pit_std=2.13).
  P(pit_signal > 0) ≈ 50% per non-cooldown step.
  Early training: agent pits every ~100-200 steps (cooldown period),
  mostly on fresh tyres (tyre_life > 0.60) → -200 penalty, NO bonus.

  For the voluntary_pit_bonus (+300) to override the -200 penalty signal,
  the agent needs 2/3 of its pits to be at tyre_life < 0.60.
  But early in training, most pits are at tyre_life > 0.60 because:
  1. Random starts can start at ANY tyre_life (including fresh)
  2. Agent pits aggressively due to high std → resets tyre_life to 1.0
     → never reaches tyre_life < 0.60 before pitting again

  Result: net gradient is strongly negative for pit_signal throughout.
  Bias: +0.006 → -0.728 (less negative than d29's -1.217 due to some
  occasional worn-tyre pits getting the +300 bonus, but still negative).

EVIDENCE FROM TRAJECTORY:
  ep_rew went from -1340 (heavy pitting everywhere) to +540 (fewer pits).
  The agent learned "pitting too often is bad" but couldn't learn
  "pitting at worn tyres is good" because the pit bias is now too negative.
  With bias=-0.728 and std=1.46: P(pit_signal>0 at worn tyre) ≈ 31%.
  → Agent sometimes pits at worn tyres but not reliably.

==============================================================================
WHAT'S NEXT (d31): BC PRE-TRAINING OF PIT ROW
==============================================================================

The fundamental problem: with near-zero bias and high pit_std, the agent
explores pitting uniformly across all tyre_life states early in training.
The negative signal from fresh-tyre pits dominates → bias goes negative.

FIX: Initialize the pit row with BC to be STATE-CONDITIONAL before PPO:
  - Collect 50 episodes of d21 driving (no pitting) to sample feature states
  - Create target: pit_signal = +2.0 when tyre_life < 0.55 (pit!)
                   pit_signal = -2.0 when tyre_life > 0.65 (don't pit!)
  - Run gradient descent on ONLY action_net.weight[2,:] and bias[2]
    (frozen features × trainable pit row = state-conditional linear classifier)
  - After BC: pit_signal > 0 at worn states, < 0 at fresh states GUARANTEED

Then PPO fine-tuning with voluntary_pit_reward=True:
  - Agent enters training with correct state-conditional pit policy
  - At worn-tyre states: pit_signal ≈ +2.0 → pit fires → +300 bonus
    → Positive advantage → gradient REINFORCES high pit_signal at worn states
  - At fresh-tyre states: pit_signal ≈ -2.0 → pit doesn't fire
    → No penalty → neutral gradient
  - No spurious fresh-tyre gradient to overcome!

This is the most principled fix. The BC targets (±2.0) are chosen because:
  - With std=2.13: P(pit_signal > 0 | mean=+2.0) ≈ 82% (usually pits)
  - P(pit_signal > 0 | mean=-2.0) ≈ 18% (rarely pits)
  After PPO: std will adjust and mean will move toward optimal timing.

ADDITIONAL FIXES:
  - Log_std[2] should also be re-initialized to 0.76 (d21's value)
    after BC, to restore exploration before PPO fine-tuning.
  - Verify BC is converging: check loss over 500 epochs, verify
    pit_signal ≈ +2.0 on a worn-tyre state and ≈ -2.0 on fresh.
