LESSONS LEARNED (D41–D46 series)
----------------------------------
1. Shortcut closure requires correct reward shaping, not just harder opponent.
   - Raising opp speed alone (D41, D43) produced positional awareness but
     created a "follow equilibrium" or made the task impossible.
   - Raising position_bonus (D44) was the key fix: broke the equilibrium
     by making being ahead 4x more valuable (+4000 vs +1000 per episode).

2. Fine-tuning trajectory: once the reward is right (D44), continued
   training monotonically improves lap completion (3→8→17).

3. Speed increases with competitive pressure:
   - D39 (opp=22): 26.91 m/s
   - D46 (opp=25, pb=2): 27.28 m/s — higher position_bonus pushes faster
   - Counter-intuitive: a harder opponent + stronger rewards → faster policy

4. Training reward ≠ eval performance (again):
   - D46 training ep_rew FELL from D45 (3440 < 3910) — low LR caused slight
     training instability, but deterministic eval jumped from 3727 → 7943.
   - The policy was fine-tuned at the behavioral level (overtake stabilization)
     in ways that training reward doesn't reflect.
