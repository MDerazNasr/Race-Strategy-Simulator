ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
-------------------
D50 = "follow equilibrium" at opp=28.

Ego speed 23.38 m/s vs opponent 28 m/s → ego is BEHIND almost always (94.3% of steps).
Position_bonus=1.0 fires only when ego is ahead. At 5.7% of steps:
  D50 position contribution = 5.7% × 2000 steps × 1.0 = ~114 reward
  D48b position contribution = 64.2% × 2000 steps × 1.0 = ~1284 reward

The 1170-reward shortfall (6122 - 4485 = 1637) is mostly explained by this gap.

Unlike D43's follow equilibrium (0% completion), D50 still completes because the
ego was already a stable driver from D48b. It just gave up on fighting for position.

Why didn't competitive pressure raise speed?
  - D44→D46 arc: multi-agent WITHOUT pits. Ego speed = throttle+braking, no other costs.
  - D50: ego WITH pits. Pitting every 460 steps creates a 100-step cooldown window.
    During those 100 steps, fresh tyres give a temporary speed burst — but at opp=28,
    the burst (~26 m/s) still can't reach the opponent (~28 m/s continuous).
  - The position_bonus isn't strong enough to overcome the speed deficit.
  - Result: agent found a stable local optimum (complete all laps + 4 pits, never fight).

PATTERN: Same as D43 (opp=25, pos_bonus=0.5 → follow) and D48b's sweet spot.
- Too high opp speed → follow equilibrium → need more position_bonus to break it
- D43 fix was: raise pos_bonus 0.5→2.0 (D44). But D44 crashed. D48b=1.0 was sweet spot.
- D50 fix: try opp=27 (smaller step) OR pos_bonus=1.5 at opp=28.

WHAT'S NEXT (D51)
-----------------
Two options:

A) D51a: opp=27, pos_bonus=1.0 (from D48b)
   - Reduce the opp speed jump: 25→27 instead of 25→28
   - D46 showed ego at 27.28 vs opp=25 → ego CAN reach 27+ m/s
   - If ego can match opp at 27 m/s, position_bonus fires more often
   - Risk: still a follow equilibrium if ego tops out at 24-25 m/s
   - Expected: speed ≈ 27 m/s, ego ahead >50%, reward > 6122

B) D51b: opp=28, pos_bonus=1.5 (from D48b, not D50)
   - Keep the faster opponent but raise the incentive to fight
   - D43→D44 showed pos_bonus is the lever to break follow equilibria
   - Risk: raising pos_bonus may destabilize like D48's 2.0
   - Expected: 1.5 between D48b=1.0 (stable) and D48=2.0 (crashed)

RECOMMENDED: D51a (opp=27, pos_bonus=1.0)
  - Smaller, safer step with cleaner causal interpretation
  - D50 proved 28 is too much; 27 is the natural next point to probe
  - Warm-start from D48b (not D50 — D50's steer std collapsed to 0.16)
